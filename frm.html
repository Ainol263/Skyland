Strawberry Farm, Laman Fig Suhanis
Address : Laman Fig Suhanis Strawberry Farm, 08100 Bedong, Kedah


It began as a hobby to fill free time, owing to hard work and persistence, the interest evolved into a means of generating family revenue. A family in Kampung Telaga Cina, Semeling, Bedong close here is now able to earn a decent living after working on a strawberry patch that is starting to bear fruit. They didn't think the plant, which is generally grown in the highlands and cold regions, could thrive in the lowlands, according to Fig Suhanis Site Manager Nur Atikah Zakaria.

She said the continuous effort due to interest, without thinking of the loss of time and money since a year ago, is now worthwhile when the crop which was only grown for fun at first is now turned into a source of family income. Since the strawberries started fruiting a month ago, the farm has become the focus of many after being infected to see and keep the crop thriving in low-lying areas. Even the unexpected fruit production is also shared with visitors so they no longer have to go elsewhere to see strawberries.

Nur Atikah said the farm was jointly managed by her cousin, Harusani Saari, and his wife, Suhada Razak who initially planted only 50 trees. Almost all of our family members have the same interest, which is to get involved in agriculture since long ago. Before this, we did a lot of planting flowers for decoration alternated with fruit trees, he said.

More than 2,000 tourists have come to observe and buy strawberries and canned goods since the strawberry farm was afflicted, he claims. Visitors traveled from all around Malaysia, including Johor, Melaka, Negeri Sembilan, and Kuala Lumpur, to see Laman Fig Suhanis' accomplishment in growing strawberries and figs. According to visitor feedback, our strawberries were distinct from other products since they were neither too sweet nor too sour. Visitors also buy the seeds of the two trees to take home, despite the fact that canned fruit is believed to be better, he said. They also seek guidance from agricultural-related authorities and make referrals in order to boost farm output.
